Employment Contracts

If you are starting a new job, your new employer may require you to get the contract notarized. You need to read the contract carefully because this is going to specify the terms, conditions, compensation, and duties associated with the role. Even though you may hire a lawyer to help you review your employment contract, you still need to get it notarized. That way, the employer knows that you have agreed to the contract.
